About

I am a PhD candidate in Statistics at the University of California, Los Angeles (2016-2021).

My research focuses on new methods, theory and tools in statistical and causal inference, enabling empirical scientists to make more transparent and robust causal claims. I am particularly interested in the inferential challenges faced by the social and health scientists, as well as the intersections of causality with machine learning and artificial intelligence.
